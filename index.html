About Christian

According to legend, his full name is Christian Blake Teller. He was born on July 6, 1947 in Bowie, Arizona, to a Navajo father and an Italian American mother. However, other accounts state that he is of Native American and German descent. Teller enlisted in the U.S. Army at the age of 17 on August 6, 1964. He was deployed to South Vietnam in September 1966, where he became a decorated war hero. He returned to the U.S. in 1967 and began training with the U.S. Army Special Forces at Fort Bragg, North Carolina under Colonel Trautman's tutelage. During a mission in November 1971 Teller's unit came under surprise attack by NVA forces. Delmore, Teller and some other surviving members were captured by the North Vietnamese. Teller managed to escape captivity in May 1972, only to be re-deployed immediately by his own request. Upon his return to the United States, Teller discovered many American civilians hated the soldiers returning from Vietnam, and was excluded from society.
